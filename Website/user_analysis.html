User Analysis

Introduction

I chose to conduct surveys instead of interviews for the user analysis phase of our project for several reasons that are more relevant to our project:

  1. Scalability: Surveys allow us to reach a larger audience more efficiently compared to interviews. Given the diverse user base of our target users, surveys enable us to gather input from a broader range of participants.
  2. Anonymity: Surveys offer a level of anonymity that interviews may not provide. Users may feel more comfortable expressing their opinions and providing honest feedback when they can do so anonymously, especially on sensitive topics related to healthcare.
  3. Flexibility: Surveys allow users to respond at their convenience, eliminating the need to co-ordinate schedules for interviews. This flexibility increases the likelihood of receiving responses.
  4. Consistency: Surveys ensure consistency in the questions asked and the data collected across all participants. This uniformity facilitates easier analysis and comparison of responses, leading to more reliable outcomes and findings.

Regarding the specifics of the survey process:

  • The survey will be distributed via product landing page that will be promoted through social media platforms such as linkedIn and twitter to healthcare and technology related target users.
  • The survey includes a mix of multiple-choice questions, rating scales, and open-ended questions to gather both quantitative and qualitative data.
  • The initial surveys presented in this report were completed with input from friends and family in a semi-interview process.

The outcomes and findings of the survey provided valuable insights into user preferences, needs, and challenges related to remote healthcare monitoring. Analysis of the survey data revealed common patterns, preferences, and areas for improvement, which informed the subsequent stages of our project development.

Summary of User Interviews

Interview 1

Purpose: Someone with experience of caring for family member who may be a potential user

Interviewee: Jennifer, near retiree (65) has elderly mother recently taken into care home, due to ailing health and ability to live alone.

Summary of Answers:

  • Is a potential user and this would have been useful whilst mother was at home.
  • No experience with health apps.
  • Most important aspects for monitoring that could have been beneficial was:
    • Monitoring whether eating
    • Medication is being taken, Recording of weight, whether showering
    • General health metrics, such as activity, heart rate, breathing.
  • Prefer to use touch based interface
  • Is open to idea of health monitoring, and would wear a smart watch.
  • Most important expectation is a user-friendly interface.

Interview 2

Purpose: Someone with experience of health apps and understanding of healthcare.

Interviewee: Rebecca (36) Comfortable with Technology, and works in IT with a background as an Occupational Therapist and working in visibility sector.

Summary of Answers:

  • Is a potential user, family member and healthcare proffesional.
  • Most crucial information for monitoring is:
    • Heart Rate
    • Steps
    • Blood Glucose
    • Blood Pressure
    • Calories intake and burned
  • Most Important features:
    • Real-time data tracking
    • Emergency Alerts
    • User-friendly Interface
  • Prefers a combination interface
  • Thinks easy-to-navigate is very important and is comfortable with integrating IoT
  • Associated to health and design well-being and Accessibily features to be included:
    • High Contrast, Large Fonts for low-vision
    • Minimalist Design
    • Inverted Text
    • Voice Over and Voice to test and voice commands
  • Main Expectations and improvements are:
    • Alerts to myself, family, healthcare proffessionals to emergencies
    • Downward health trends
    • Insights into the data
    • Ability to share data
    • Accuracy of the data
